DISORDER IS A COLLECTION BASED ON THE IDEA OF NEUTRALITY AND RAW MINIMALISM, WITH THE PURPOSE OF CREATING CLOTHES THAT CAN SOMEHOW BE COMPARED TO PIECE OF ART BY THEIR UNIQUENESS.

EVERYDAY LOOKS ARE REINVENTED AND RESHAPED TO FIT NEW TYPES OF IDENTITY. INDEED, DISORDER IS MEANT TO FREE PEOPLE FROM SOCIAL CHAINS AND ENABLES THEM TO SHOW WHO THEY TRULY ARE. IT ENHANCES ATTITUDE RATHER THAN GENDER, GIVING AN INIMITABLE TASTE TO EVERY GARMENT AS THE PAINTER DOES WITH EVERY CANVAS.

THE JAPANESE CANVAS IS IDEED THE MAIN FABRIC CHOSEN FOR DISORDER, IN REMINDER NOT ONLY OF ART BUT ALSO OF THE NEUTRALITY OF ITS ORIGINAL OFF-WHITE COLOUR, WHICH DOESN’T HAVE ANY SEXUAL CONNOTATION. DISORDER TRANSGRESSES RULES BY CREATING CONFUSION, FADING STEREOTYPES AND BLURRING THE COMMONLY ACCEPTED CONCEPT OF THE GENDER.
